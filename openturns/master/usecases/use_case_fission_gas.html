Fission gas releaseÂ¶

Fuel performance codes [1] like TRANSURANUS [2, 3] predict the behavior of nuclear fuel rods, such as the temperature, internal pressure, and fission gas release. This use case presents several simplified Gaussian process surrogate models for fission gas release fitted to training data generated with TRANSURANUS. Fission gas release is expressed as a fraction of what is created by the fission reaction. They are derived from the work by [robertson2024].

Each of the models has two inputs:

  • Single atom diffusion multiplier denoted by .

  • Fraction released due to micro-cracking, denoted by .

Each model corresponds to different sets of experimental conditions. The number of different sets of environmental conditions is denoted by , and in the following we use the index () to identify a given set of environmental conditions.

For each set of environmental conditions , fission gas release was measured, and the measured value is denoted by . The measured values are extracted from the International Fuel Performance Experiments (IFPE) database. Measurement uncertainty is represented by a normal distribution with mean , and its standard deviation is a known function of :

The model corresponding to the environmental conditions is denoted by . The values of the single atom diffusion multiplier and of the fraction of gas released due to micro-cracking corresponding to the measured are unobserved.
